I got to be honest, as much as I like the fact that this small island nation managed to qualify for the World Cup after what is 52 staggering years I barely recognize anyone. Apart from the 2 Premier League players in Isidor and Bellegarde from Sunderland and Wolves respectively. The former being part of a Sunderland team that just qualified to Europe in their first season back in the Premier League which is an astonishing achievement given the history of promoted clubs going straight back down and the latter being part of a Wolves side who were confirmed to be relegated so long ago. So this might be the only team I just go with reputation really 😅.

There could be some underrated people from this squad but I barely know anything about them.

# The lineup

Seems like the coach prefers another classic in the 4-2-3-1 with Bellegarde being part of the pivot and Isidor either being the main striker or the CAM behind the striker which isn't a bad place to be.

From what I was able to gather their main captain and keeper in Placide who is 38 years old is currently carrying an injury so the backup in Pierre would probably take the sticks who was part of a Sochaux team that got promoted to the second division of French football in the season gone past and he kept 9 clean sheets in that timeframe so he should really be taking the sticks for this country really and he has a big opportunity.

Coming up to the defense Hannes Delcroix seems to be one of the lock in players at CB. He apparently used to play for Burnley in the Premier League but are going back down and he jumped ship to Lugano in January this year and has put up steady appearances for them as they finished 3rd in the Swiss League season which gives them Conference league football. Not bad. As for the other spot I think that will mostly go to Duverne who has had a good season for Gent in the Belgian league really with 21 appearances for them on loan popping up with a goal and assist too. He seems versatile and can play both CB and RB too so it's a good headache to have. If Duverne really slots in at CB then I think the RB spot should be going to Carlens Arcus from Angers in Ligue 1. Only 3 assists in 28 games yes but it's better than fitting a CB at RB right? The LB spot seems to be on lock by a Martin Expérience(Cracking name btw) from Nancy who are in Ligue 2 in France and finished 14th this season.

Now to the midfield, like I said before Bellegarde is locked on one of the spots in the midfield pivot in this formation and as for the other spot you got some options. Leverton Pierre started their last friendly but honestly his numbers were not that fantastic and he was in the second division of Portugal but I think a better option should definitely be Danley Jean Jacques who plays for Philadelphia Union in the MLS. He has had a good couple seasons with them scoring a total of 6 goals and 2 assists across both seasons he's played with them. The team as a whole topped their Conference table but lost in the knockouts to NYCFC but this year hasn't been great with them plum last at the moment. Still think he should get the nod next to Bellegarde.

Alright, the frontline. Isidor is naturally going to be there for sure either upfront or behind the actual striker or even on the right wing if necessary. He has that kind of versatility since switching his allegiance from France to Haiti in March of this year(Couldn't have declared earlier than that mate? 😅). As for going for people who consistently got gametime I see the likes of Ruben Providence who plays in the second division of the Netherlands and can play either on the left or right honestly. 3 goal contributions in 30 overall games played is a good enough return for a country as small as Haiti let's be real(I know I cannot chat when my country India can even produce someone like even this 😢). As for the right side it seems like a no contest with Josué Casimir for Auxerre in Ligue 1 who has had 31 appearances this season with a solitary goal to his name. And as for the main striker to lead the line it would appear to be Lenny Joseph who has had a very good season for Ferenvaros in Hungary as they qualified for the Conference League this season and he has put up 16 goals in total with a couple even in the Champions League which isn't half bad.
