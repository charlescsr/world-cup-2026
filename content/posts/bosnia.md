Just like last time out in the 22 World Cup I will go through what I know about the players I remember from the bigger leagues and try to be more closer to how the coach will really pick the lineup as much as I can.

My ones to watch from this Bosnia squad are actually two people and one was a pleasant surprise to see:

## Amar Dedic

![d447455a120b88ebcb233083363b5a02.png](images/d447455a120b88ebcb233083363b5a02.png)

Someone who did numbers when I used him in my Manchester United save back in Football Manager 24(Just will abbreviate it to FM after this because it may appear quite a lot of times 😅). And he has finally found his footing in the football world after his time at RB Salzburg. I know he can't get a move after this tournament since he only moved to Benfica in the Portuguese league this season but he has put up very steady numbers for Benfica as well under the Special One Jose. His starts are less at 21 out of 24 in the league since it would appear he is behind Alexander Bah for the most part. To be fair he has been a mainstay for his national team at RB and I think he is someone to watch at 23.

## Kerim Alajbegović

![4b0c5c99bfbe7bd121a0fa56fc0df791.png](images/4b0c5c99bfbe7bd121a0fa56fc0df791.png)

Now this one was a surprise because I did have this guy in my Manchester United career mode when I first started playing FM26(Given how United are performing IRL is anyone really surprised at how I'm coping with these career modes? 😅) and he was actually German in that save game and was a very good player for me. Quality winger but seemed to be played as more of a CM who could drift to the left perhaps from what I'm seeing for Salzburg. He is such a solid wonderkid who has already gotten his move in the summer with him returning to Bayer Leverkusen in the Bundesliga after starting his career there in Koln and the latter. So I'm honestly happy for the guy. He is someone who is one to watch for sure if he gets more gametime in the tournament. Salzburg did not have the best season in the Austrian Bundesliga given they finished third behind Sturm Graz and eventual champions LASK. Given they are backed by the Red Bull group it always is a surprise when they don't win but Kerim was solid getting 9 goals and 3 assists in 28 matches which is a decent return for the 18 year old. Hoping he gets minutes.

# The lineup

From what I have searched on this team they play your bog standard classic 4-4-2 which I can respect. It was the formation that knocked Italy out after all(I will stop the digs promise😂). The goalies are slim pickings really. The main keeper the coach seems to trust just got relegated with St. Pauli from the Bundesliga but he's the tallest so we'll run with that. Dedic and Muharemovic are lock ins at RB and CB respectively after good steady seasons for their teams in Benfica and Sassuolo. The other CB looks likely to be Katic after he was part of the Schalke side that has arrived back in the Bundesliga. The LB still seems to be Sead Kolasinac. A 32 year old Sead Kolasinac. I remember the guy from the banter Arsenal days(Don't come at me Arsenal fans you're champions I respect that). It seems like there aren't any other options.

Coming to midfield there are some problems with 2 injuries to Hadziahmetovic and Sunjic. I did think about Tahirovic but he seems to have had a very average season for Brondby but I think it is a close call. He may only come off the bench. Gigovic who plays for Young Boys had a slightly better season in the Swiss League(There was an absolutely mental story where a recently promoted team won the league this season in their first season back in the top league) so I think I will pair him beside Ivan Basic who had a good season with Astana in the Kazakh league.As for the width I think Memic is hard to overlook with 8 assists and 3 goals and given you're playing a 4-4-2 you need an assisting winger than a goal scoring one like Kerim so I kind of understand the coach playing Memic on the left. The left seems to be taken by PSV winger Bajraktarevic who has for the most part come from the bench given he's only had 8 starts out of 28 matches for PSV but still returned decently well with 4 goals and 4 assists. Coming to the attack Demirovic from Stuttgart is a lock in at ST for sure. You may think I'm looking at values alone but Demirovic is actually a Bundesliga veteran and he is still 28. He was at Freiburg a couple years, Augsburg a couple years and now he's at Stuttgart for two years now(Wonder which club he'll go to next 🤔). And of course you can't ignore the captain himself in Edin Dzeko.
