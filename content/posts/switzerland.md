Got to say that I am seeing quite a bit of the old guard when I remember this Swiss team from the last World Cup but I do notice some people to keep an eye on for this tournament for sure. Yakin has done wonders with this team with one of their notable achievements being knocking out the defending champions in Italy in the Round of 16 in Euro 2024 after finishing second in their group but we will definitely miss Xherdan Shaqiri and his beefy thigh muscles for sure. We salute you mate.

Alright down to the ones to watch I found 2 who look very promising indeed:

Someone who not only seems to be under the radar given I don't follow the Bundesliga a lot but also someone who looks so versatile. Who can play almost anywhere in midfield and the attack as you can see here:

![565870289a0f966cca12a9ce8d0a1113.png](/images/565870289a0f966cca12a9ce8d0a1113.png)

## Johan Manzambi

A European finalist in what was his team Freiburg's first ever European final and they lost out to Aston Villa(Let's be honest the Europa League is just the Unai Emery League at this point because he's won it 5 times) doesn't take away from his good season with 5 goals and 4 assists in 27 matches for Freiburg this season and it's being vindicated with the call up to the national team for this tournament and there is an opportunity with him with Ndoye out injured at the moment. ## The lineup

From what I am able to see Yakin has two variations of playing. One is a classic 4-3-3 to be more free in the attacking sense and a 3-4-3 to be more pragmatic against the bigger teams. Given their group contains Bosnia, Qatar and Canada I feel like the 3-4-3 will mostly be saved just for the knockouts.

Down to the players, I feel like most seem to be reputation picks rather than pure stats but some picks can be biased by me. Just a fan okay 😅

Okay, in goal I think you can never look past Gregor Kobel who has been very very consistent for Borussia Dortmund. Despite Marvin Keller being young I honestly don't think he gets a look in until Kobel retires.

Down to the defense I think you cannot look past Elvedi who has been a steady stalwart for Monchengladbach and Akanji who has been part of a double winning Inter Milan side with the Serie A and Coppa Italia in his loan spell from Man City which is weird to even think about. If you're looking for an additional CB I think Denis Zakaria is a capable option who can be at both DM and also CB. So he is useful to have for sure and he had a decent season for Monaco in Ligue 1 with 24 matches at a 7 rating in Fotmob which is not bad.

Coming to the full backs I think Muheim seems like a better option than Ricardo Rodriguez who mostly may be better suited to play CB than LWB. The former had a decent season for Hamburger SV as they stayed in the Bundesliga after getting promotion the previous year. And as for the right side it would appear the Widmer is their best option really. Sometimes Aebischer plays there too but he did not have a fantastic time on loan out to Pisa who got relegated from Serie A. Coming to the frontline I think 2 lock ins with Ndoye out injured will definitely be Ruben Vargas who had a decent chunk of gametime but okayish returns with 3 goals and 6 assists for Sevilla and also Breel Embolo even if his returns have just been 8 goals and 3 assists for Rennes. I feel like Itten deserves a start given he has put numbers for Fortuna Dusseldorf in the second division of German football with a tally of 15 goals and 3 assists but he could not prevent the relegation of his team however. I think I am sticking with Embolo given he has repaid the faith of the national team when he got picked in the Euros last time out.
