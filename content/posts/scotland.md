Scotland. Or as I like to call them the "Scott Mcclutch" country. They made their qualification after thumping Denmark 4-2 with a Mctominay bicycle kick and a Tierney wonder goal got them through to this tournament. Looking at the squad the only weaknesses I see are in defense and the goalie because one of their keepers is 43 years old. I'll get into that when discussing the lineup. As for the ones to watch there isn't many. The youngsters in the team barely have made an impact in their club seasons and it seems that the coach just relies on his experienced core of players which I do get.

Alright down to the lineup and the beginning is going to hurt a bit to write about

# The lineup

Here's where I have my gripes with this squad. Goalies. You got Angus Gunn who is 30 and plays for Nottingham Forest in the Premier League which ain't bad. And the remaining two in Liam Kelly who is 30 and Craig Gordon who is said 43 year old keeper play for Rangers and Hearts respectively in the Scottish league which is fair enough. Could you guess how many games they have played for their respective clubs this season?

A grand total of 7 games in total between the 3 😑. Angus Gunn may seem like the best option to pick and the tallest but he has played just ONE FREAKING game for Forest in the Premier League when Forest were struggling to stay up. And even that was just 45 minutes and that was it. When the keepers switched between Stefan Ortega and Matz Sels he barely ever got a look in and with John Victor as another keeper option they honestly had things covered. As for the other two they have played 3 games each for their respective clubs and I honestly just want to avoid the 43 year old and go for the one from Rangers. Even if he's shorter. I feel like the manager may pick between Gunn and Gordon though.

Alright down to the defense I think this is a solid place but Andy Robertson isn't the guy he was before and it showed with him barely getting any playtime this season for Liverpool and he has been confirmed to leave at the end of the season as well but I think given he's captain for the national team he is a lock on at LB for sure. Down to the CBs I think given you're in a group with Morocco and Brazil you may need 3 CBs for sure compared to just 2. It may allow the fullbacks to have the freedom to push forward more really. I think Scott Mckenna is a definite lock in as one of them. He's had a very solid season for Dinamo Zagreb all the way out in Croatia which feels like rogue signing by them. The next one weirdly is actually Kieran Tierney who was part of a title winning Celtic side and really changed things up ever since he joined them on a free from Arsenal and his numbers have been solid too with 6 goals and 12 assists in a total of 53 games overall in his season. And for the final CB I think it would have to go to John Souttar from Rangers who has consistent gametime for them this season. Did not pop up with goals but was steady for them.

Alright to the midfield and I feel like 4 spots are already locked in. First is Billy Gilmour who has slowed down in terms of his gametime at Napoli with only 8 starts in 17 matches but I think Scotland require the talent and he is most definitely one of them at 24. The next one is Lewis Ferguson from Bologna who has made 45 appearances and did score one goal in the league from deep and is someone who can sit in front of the defense for sure. The third spot is of course the big man himself Scott Mctominay. The man who is seriously adored by Napoli fans and he's backed it up this season too with a total of 14 goals and 3 assists as a midfielder in 44 matches total. The final spot is John Mcginn. Come on the guy recently was instrumental in Aston Villa winning the Europa League and had an assist in the final too. He also played 44 matches in total delivering 10 goals and 7 assists. Not a bad showing.

I do realize with adding Ferguson in I have no space for either Hickey or Patterson but after checking their gametime and numbers I am sticking with this setup. They can come off the bench to close the game out.

Now to the frontline. This is a little conundrum cause you have room for 2 strikers. One of them could be Scott Mctominay if you want to fit Hickey in but I think you need someone combative to give Scott his freedom so that's why I'm going with Ferguson. To the strikers you have Ché Adams who plays for Torino of all places and has scored 8 goals and 2 assists in 36 games. Next is Lyndon Dykes who started at Birmingham City and moved to Charlton Athletic in January and scored a total of 5 goals and 3 assists over both clubs. The remaining two are Lawrence Shankland who was on fire for Hearts in the Scottish League as they almost ended a very long Celtic reign over the league with 20 goals and 4 assists in a total of 34 games. And finally George Hirst who plays for Ipswich and was a decent part in their pursuit of promotion and will play in the Premier League next season for sure and he returned with 11 goals and 3 assists in 44 games total.

I think looking at it as a whole Shankland is a definite lock in. Now it's down to who you want as the second striker. Someone who can drop a bit deep like Adams or another out and out striker in Hirst. You know what let's give it to Hirst and see what he can do. Adams can take the bench.
