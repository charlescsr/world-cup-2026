The smallest nation to ever qualify for the World Cup and they have done it for the very first time in their entire history. They topped their group in the qualifiers so they more than deserve this really and they have got a fairly legendary manager in Dick Advocaat leading them after a bit of hiatus actually. The story is a big different. So after helping them qualify Advocaat decided to step down actually due to his daughter not feeling well which was absolutely understandable. And once his daughter's health improved the team did not gel well at all with a couple losses in friendly matches and that along with pressure from an airline that was sponsoring this team wanted Advocaat back and he was happy to return. All's well that ends well 😄.

As for the ones to watch it was hard to bring 2 to the table because it was hard picking a second one but I think I managed to get them. Here are the two I'd like to see play for them in this tournament:

## Deveron Fonville

![aa35920f3ba1897a1ee86b65ab73cb79.png](/images/aa35920f3ba1897a1ee86b65ab73cb79.png)

Coming off a fantastic season for N.E.C Nijmegen in the Eredivisie where they managed to get into the qualification rounds for the Champions League and he managed to get very consistent gametime with a total of 34 games in all competitions and chimed in with 2 assists too. Not bad for someone who is versatile to play both CB and LB.

Let's see if he will be given minutes or not.

Now the second one was a bit of a toss up between two wingers. Arjany Martha who plays for Rotherham United in League One and Jeremy Antonisse who plays in Greece for Kifisia but I think I'm going for the latter.

## Jeremy Antonisse

![721ad428d438c0a0dc1aa93e6066df8c.png](/images/721ad428d438c0a0dc1aa93e6066df8c.png)

I think the only reason why I am going for this guy is because he had very consistent gametime in the Greek Super League this season and he seemed to deliver well for the national team when called upon this year as well which is not bad to really see. Of course his output could be better with just 1 goal and 2 assists but it is a very close call between him and Martha from League One really. Let's see who gets the nod in the frontline.

## The lineup

Looking at the goalies it feels like their oldest one Eloy Room is the one that has played consistently for their respective clubs even if it was Miami FC so it feels like I will have to pick to him really. Coming to the defence it feels like Tyrese Noslin is a good pick for RB after he had a fairly good season for Telstar chipping in with 3 goals and 4 assists in 39 games for them but he primarily has played as part of a back 3 than a back 4 so I would understand if Advocaat goes for Shurandy Sambo instead who played on loan for Sparta Rotterdam from Burnley. As for the CBs I see mostly Roshon van Ejima and Armando Obispo play as the duo at the back. I do hope Fonville dislodges either of them. Now down to LB it feels like it may be Sherel Floranus who has started quite a significant amount of games for PEC Zwolle in the Netherlands. He has returned with 4 assists and 1 goal in 34 total games for them which is not bad.

Alright coming to the midfield and I feel like the Bacuna brothers are most definitely a lock in as 2 of the midfield spots for sure. Leandro the older is well known from his time in the Premier League with Aston Villa and plays for Igdir which is in the second division of Turkish football and has put up numbers for them with 9 goals and 4 assists in 35 total matches in the league alone. The younger Juninho plays on loan at FC Volendam and is most known for playing for Birhimham City in England as well. He didn't play as constantly really only making 15 appearances with 2 assists but I think you cannot look past both Bacunas for sure. The last spot in midfield is a tussle between Kevin Felida who plays for FC Den Bosch and Godfried Roemeratoe who plays for RKC Waalwijk and both those clubs are in the second division of Dutch football but it does feel like a no contest when you see Felida having 4 goals and 3 assists in 34 matches compared to just 2 assists from Roemeratoe so I think that settles that.

Now the frontline. This was surprising but Tahith Chong is part of this squad. He started out as a Dutch youth national but he later on accepted a call up from Curacao in 2021 but they had to withdraw due to a COVID outbreak and his nationality change was confirmed in 2025. He did not have the best season for Sheffield United in the Championship with just 1 assist in 22 games which is low but I think he has the quality that will serve Curacao well. As for the left hand side it would be Jearl Margeritha who has scored 7 goals and 12 assists all the way down in the 4th division of Belgian football which was news to me as well. And with Kenji Gorre racing to be fit I don't see anyone else to fill that spot as of yet. To the strikers you have 3 options. The most notable one here is Jurgen Locadia who is well known from his time at PSV in the Dutch League and he has had a fairly big journeyman level career and finally settling at Miami FC as a free agent in what may be the second division of football in the USA(I'm calling it that don't ever chat to me that it's soccer 😠) but he's only scored 5 goals and 2 assists in 12 total matches but the other options aren't fantastic either. Gervane Kastaneer plays for Terengganu in the Malysian Liga Super and has scored 4 goals and 1 assist in 13 matches for them. Brandley Kuwas for Volendam has been better actually with 7 goals and 5 assists in 37 total matches so that may be my pick at the moment.
