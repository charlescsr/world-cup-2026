Alright South Korea. I only knew Heung-Min Son back in the day because of his time in the Premier League and he has been fairly decent in the MLS for LAFC too with 12 goals and 3 assists in 13 matches last year in the season as they made the knockouts in 2025 but lost out to eventual finalists Vancouver Whitecaps. But things haven't been fantastic in 2026 with them down in 7th compared to 3rd last year and his output is only 8 assists in 12 matches but given his reputation and that he's captain you don't see him not starting in this team.

Anyways, down to the ones to watch I managed to find 2 that are young and are coming off of good seasons for their clubs and they are:

## Hyun-Jun Yang
![abd70903b04515962aa2f883617581d5.png](/images/abd70903b04515962aa2f883617581d5.png)

Coming off the back of a big season for him with Celtic who very recently broke hearts and the team too(There is literally a team called Hearts that were leading till the final day before being beaten by Celtic for them to win the title) and his starts were consistent by playing 28 of the 31 matches he took a part of and returning with 8 goals and 1 assist. He's got a while left since he's only 23 and maybe he can be more settled in Celtic and Scotland and I do hope to see the likes of him get minutes because it was hard finding ones to watch in this squad 😅

## Jens Castrop
![bcf666ee523120f2635cbb737925a44e.png](/images/bcf666ee523120f2635cbb737925a44e.png)

And he has had a fairly solid season for Monchengladbach with 20 starts out of 26 matches out of a possible 34 and a 6.87 rating according to Fotmob but his returns are only at 3 goals and 1 assist coming from LWB since he seems to play there for Gladbach and the system is very similar to what the coach does here too so maybe he could be someone who could slot in there for them. Maybe his output could lead to him getting benched but let's see if he'll get minutes.

## The lineup

So it would appear the coach prefers "The Amorim". Named after the coach who really made a name for himself with this formation at Sporting primarily and it is a capable formation with 3 defenders and width provided by just the wingbacks. As for the lineup choices I was befuddled to put it best 😅. It was hard finding the right players but I am just going by stats since I barely watch any korean players bar Son during his Premier League times.

To the keepers there are 3 choices and despite him leaning on the oldest one in the 3 with Seung-gyu Kim I'd rather just look at the youngest one who is Bum-keun Song who is tall and had a good season with Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors FC with 7 clean sheets out of 15 matches and 12 goals conceded in the current ongoing season for them. I'm going with him as the pick. The others are just too old man.

Coming to defense we have our first problem. Kim Min-jae is currently injured and it is hard to tell if he will be back in time for the tournament start but I will definitely ignore him till I know more by the time their matches come around. In his stead for the 3 CBs I see Han-beom Lee who has had a solid season for FC Midtjylland starting 25 out of 29 matches as they finished second only to AGF in the Danish league. The next CB would appear to be Lee Gi-Hyuk who is having a solid season for Gangwon FC in the Korean league this year(One thing I am finding different to understand that Japan and Korea's league seasons are in a single year). And the final one would appear to be Tae-Hyeon Kim who is again looking solid for Kashima Antlers in the and I kid you not:

Anyway that is the defense sorted out. Coming to the midfield there is another injury problem with In-beom Hwang who has been injured since March it would seem so he may not start just yet. I think one of the midfielders looks like Seung-Ho Paik who has been really solid for Birmingham City in the Championship helping them finish in 10th in their first season after being promoted out of League 1(They are backed by Tom Brady so it is kind of to be expected really for them to perform well) and his midfield partner seems to be Kim Jin-gyu from Jeonbuk in Korea who is having a good season so far. And as for the wingback options I think Tae-Seok Lee from Austria Wien is a little one to watch but the returns aren't exactly fantastic but his rating is more solid. 3 goals and 3 assists in 30 matches as they finished 4th isn't half bad given he only moved this year to Austria. So that is my pick for LWB and RWB will be Seol Young-Woo from Red Star Belgrade who has been very solid with 2 goals and 5 assists in 31 matches as they won the Serbian league this season which is great to see.

Looking at the attack I think 2 people who are always a lock in are Son at ST and Kang-in Lee from PSG at CAM. The remaining CAM spot is between Jae-sung Lee who has had an average season for Mainz in the Bundesliga and the one to watch Hyun-Jun Yang from Celtic and I'm siding with my pick from Celtic.
